Chicken 65

This fiery South Indian appetizer consists of bite-sized chicken pieces marinated in a bold mix of red chilies, ginger, and garlic, then deep-fried to a golden crisp. Tempered with fresh curry leaves and green chilies, it offers an explosion of spicy and tangy flavors. With its crunchy exterior and juicy interior, Chicken 65 is an addictive snack that perfectly sets the stage for a grand meal.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Curry leaves
  • Curd
  • Oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Let it marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Add the corn flour and rice flour to the marinated chicken and mix until the pieces are evenly coated.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. Deep fry the chicken pieces in batches over medium-high heat until they are crispy and golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  4. For the tempering: In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil.
  5. Add chopped garlic, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Saut√© for a few seconds until fragrant.
  6. Toss the fried chicken pieces into the tempering pan and mix well to coat them in the spicy oil.
  7. Serve hot as an appetizer with a squeeze of lemon.
